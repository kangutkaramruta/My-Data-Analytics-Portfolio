# Project: MarketCo Marketing Analytics (Assignment 2)

## Goal
Analysed customer behaviour, membership performance (Gold vs Non-Gold), communication patterns (inbound/outbound), and demographic drivers of spend to identify marketing opportunities and recommend strategies to improve online sales and retention.

## Dataset & Scope
Used customer-level data including membership status, product/contract spend, purchase frequency, tenure, inbound/outbound communications over 24 and 60 months, demographics (age, gender), technology preferences (browser/OS), and geography (Australian states). Also identified missing web analytics data required to explain high bounce rate and low conversion.

## Key Problems Solved

### Gold vs Non-Gold membership performance gap

- Compared purchase frequency, average spend, total spend, and communication behaviour by membership type.

- Identified Gold membership underperformance: lower purchase frequency and lower average spend compared to non-gold segments, suggesting weak renewal/engagement.

### Customer spend drivers by age + communication effectiveness

- Analysed spending distribution across age bands and linked it to inbound/outbound communication patterns.

- Found customers aged 56+ (especially 75+) spend the most, yet receive relatively fewer outbound communications than younger groups, indicating misaligned targeting.

### Behaviour patterns by product category, tenure, tech, and location

- Evaluated which product areas drive the highest total spend and longer tenure.

- Identified travel commissions as the strongest performer (high spend + long tenure), while Gold membership had the lowest spend and tenure.

- Analysed spending differences by Australian state (NSW highest, TAS lowest).

- Checked seasonal behaviour near Valentine’s Day & Mother’s Day with minimal overall changes (slight lift in cruise-related products near Valentine’s).

### What data is missing to diagnose bounce + conversion

- Recommended adding web analytics variables: exit page, time on page, click paths, referral sources, conversions, bounce events, and cookie/session behaviour.

- Proposed A/B testing to identify which website design/content changes improve conversion.

- Proposed social data collection and sentiment analysis for customer attitude signals.

## Methods Used

- Descriptive analytics and segmentation (membership, age, gender, product, state)

- Aggregations: purchase frequency, average/total spend, tenure comparisons

- Communication analysis: inbound vs outbound over 24/60 months

- Marketing measurement thinking: retention vs acquisition lens

- Proposed experimental design: A/B testing

- Proposed text analytics: sentiment analysis (social feedback)

## Recommendations Delivered

### Recommendation 1: Fix Gold membership retention & value

- Introduce limited-time discounted sign-up, and renewal perks (e.g., exclusive benefits + discounted renewal).

- Improve Gold value proposition by offering incentives on high-value categories (e.g., travel commissions) such as early access, member-only deals.

- Improve outbound targeting using behavioural triggers (cart reminders, similar-product recommendations).

- Investigate drivers of higher inbound communication from non-gold users (complaints vs enquiries vs support needs) and resolve friction points.

### Recommendation 2: Run age-targeted campaigns + strengthen web data

- Prioritise personalised campaigns for 56+ high spenders to maintain and grow revenue.

- For younger segments, test price-sensitive offers and alternative engagement strategies (since higher outbound contact is not translating into higher spend).

- Implement web analytics tracking and traffic source analysis to pinpoint conversion drop-offs.

- Consider product bundling (combo deals/upgrades) to retain travel/cruise customers.

## Outcome

Delivered a customer-segmentation and communication effectiveness assessment, identified underperforming membership economics (Gold), highlighted high-value customer segments (older age groups + travel/cruise), and proposed a data roadmap (web analytics + A/B testing + sentiment analysis) to address high bounce rate and low conversion.
